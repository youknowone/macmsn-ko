Receiving messages from unknown senders

A message that is sent to you by someone who is not on your contact list or the My Allow List might be junk mail. The following table explains your options when Messenger alerts you that you received a message from an unknown sender.

Caution

Never disclose your password, credit card number, social security number, or other personal information in an instant message conversation. To help prevent infection by malicious software, never accept or open any file or link in an instant message until you verify its authenticity with the sender.

Option

Security implications

Accept

If you recognize the e-mail address of the person trying to contact you, you can accept the message. After you accept a message, the conversation window opens and you can add the person as a contact by clicking the Add Contact link.

If you accept a message by mistake and then decide that the message is junk, click Block in the conversation window. This blocks that person from seeing your online status or contacting you again.

Decline

If the e-mail address is unfamiliar, you can decline the message. This closes the conversation window. The sender does not receive notification that the message was declined.

Preferences

You can change your preferences so that Messenger blocks all messages from unknown senders. Or, you can turn off warnings from Messenger when you receive a message from an unknown sender.

Note Declining a message from an unknown sender does not block the sender. To block a sender, you must add the sender as a contact, and then block the contact.
