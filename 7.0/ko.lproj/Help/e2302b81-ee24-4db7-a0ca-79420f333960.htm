Use Bonjour to find nearby Messenger for Mac users

Show All

Bonjour, which is included in Mac OS X, makes it possible for computers on a network to detect one another. If you turn on Bonjour in Messenger, your contact list includes other Messenger for Mac Bonjour users on your network who are online and located near you. You can send and receive messages with Bonjour users as you do with other contacts.

Important

If you select the Allow local Bonjour users to see me in their contact lists check box, other people on your network can see your e-mail address and online statusAlso known as presence, a system for contacts to broadcast their availability to other contacts. Without sending a message, you can see that a contact might be busy, offline, or online and available to communicate..

  1. Sign in to your personal accountAn account on the Windows Live network, including all e-mail addresses ending in "@live.com", "@msn.com", and "@hotmail.com". With a personal account you can communicate with other contacts on the Windows Live and Yahoo networks. or corporate accountAn account on a corporate network that uses the Office Communications Server 2007. With a corporate account, you can communicate with other people in your network and in other federated networks. You can also use a corporate account to communicate with contacts on the Windows Live, MSN, MSN Hotmail, Yahoo, and AOL networks..

  2. On the Contact menu, click Privacy.

  3. Under Bonjour Settings, select the Show local Bonjour users in my contact list check box.

    To make yourself visible to other users, select the Allow local Bonjour users to see me in their contact lists check box.

Notes 

  • After you turn on Bonjour, nearby Messenger for Mac users appear in your contact list in the Bonjour Users group.

  • To add a Bonjour user as a contact, in the contact list under Bonjour Users, drag a name to another group in your contact list.
